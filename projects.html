Binary Classification Using Some Notions Of Kernelized Data Depths

Supervised by Dr. Subhajit Dutta, Department of Mathematics and Statistics, IIT Kanpur

project report, presentation
Abstract: This project explores the use of kernelized data depth functions for binary... classification. It demonstrates how these functions can capture multi-modality in data clouds, which traditional depth functions like half-space and spatial depth cannot. Two such novel depth measures—sphere depth and kernelized spatial depth—derived from kernelized half-space and spatial depth are introduced which captures the multi-modality of the data cloud. These depth measures are used as features for classification, capturing data structure in a non-parametric manner. The project compares the effectiveness of these depth-based features in binary classification tasks using machine learning algorithms such as support vector machines, K-nearest neighbors, and generalized additive models. It simulates data from four location and scale models, including multivariate normal and t distributions, builds classifiers based on depth values, and evaluates their performance on training and test data.

Comparative Analysis Of Out-Of-Distribution Detection Methods: A Literature Review and Experimental Evaluation

Supervised by Ms. Lakshmi Annamalai, Sc 'E', Center for Artificial Intelligence and Robotics (CAIR), DRDO, Bengaluru

project report
Abstract: Deep neural networks (DNNs) achieve high accuracy in tasks like speech... recognition and image classification but struggle with predictive uncertainty and detecting out-of-distribution (OOD) samples, which impacts their performance in real-world applications. Ensuring AI systems can handle OOD examples and flag those requiring human intervention is crucial, especially in safety-critical domains such as medicine and finance. In this project I evaluated several OOD detection methods e.g. Mahalanobis method, KL-Matching, energy/entropy based methods etc. on state-of-the-art datasets. Compared these methods with the method developed by CAIR. Assessed the competence of different OOD detection methods in identifying non-believable samples.

Pairs Trading: A Strategy Exploration

Under the guidance of Dr. Amit Mitra, Department of Mathematics and Statistics, IIT Kanpur

project report
Abstract: Pairs Trading is an arbitrage technique used in the stock market to exploit... short-term mispricing between similar stocks. This project focuses on identifying stock pairs that exhibit a long-term price relationship, typically moving together due to similar exposure to risks or common underlying economic factors. By analyzing historical data and identifying these co-integrated stocks, we can develop a trading strategy that capitalizes on temporary price divergences. When one stock in a pair is undervalued and the other is overvalued, a long position is taken on the undervalued stock and a short position on the overvalued one, with the expectation that prices will revert to their historical relationship. The project aims to identify co-integrated stocks from major economic sectors, build a trading strategy using moving averages, and optimize the threshold for trading signals to maximize profits. The strategy is tested to estimate potential profitability.

Analysis Of Indian Web Series

Under the guidance of Dr. Dootika Vats, Department of Mathematics and Statistics, IIT Kanpur

project report, github
Abstract: The goal of this project is to gather insightful information about the platforms,... genres, and preferences of the Indian audience for web shows. By analyzing collected data, we aim to understand the target audience of streaming platforms, identify the most popular genres among Indian viewers, and discern patterns in show releases tailored for this audience. Our conclusions are based purely on data visualizations created in our Shiny App, as no statistical analysis is performed. This approach will provide a clear picture of audience preferences and streaming platform strategies.
